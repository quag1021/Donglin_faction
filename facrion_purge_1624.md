Event: The Donglin Memorial (June 1624)
Yang Lian, a leading Donglin censor, initiates a structural challenge against the Eastern Depot by submitting a memorial detailing the "Twenty-Four Crimes" of Wei Zhongxian. The objective is to leverage the Tianqi Emperor to dismantle the eunuch apparatus. To steelman Wei's position: the Donglin faction is attempting to monopolize the bureaucracy and cut off the Emperor's independent intelligence network. The Emperor ignores the memorial, revealing the Donglin network's critical vulnerability—they rely entirely on orthodox administrative channels, while Wei controls the physical flow of information to the throne.

Event: The Liaodong Bribe Narrative (1625)
Rather than engaging in a purely ideological debate, Wei Zhongxian executes a political maneuver against the Donglin's peripheral alliances. Following the military failure in Liaodong, Wei's faction accuses Donglin-aligned commander Xiong Tingbi of offering bribes to Donglin scholars (the "Six Heroes") to avoid execution. This reframes an ideological dispute as an issue of massive financial corruption. This provides the legal mechanism for the Eastern Depot to bypass standard judicial review, effectively neutralizing the Donglin's bureaucratic influence.

Event: The Purge of the "Six Heroes" (Late 1625)
With the corruption narrative firmly established, the Eastern Depot arrests the core Donglin leadership, including Yang Lian and Zuo Guangdou. They are subjected to severe interrogation and die within the imperial prison. The balance of power decisively shifts. The Donglin academies are ordered to be demolished nationwide, physically erasing their institutional network. Wei Zhongxian achieves total administrative capture, forcing the surviving bureaucracy into absolute compliance.
